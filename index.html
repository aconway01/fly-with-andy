Discovery Flights

A Discovery Flight is the ideal first step into aviation — a hands-on, low-pressure introduction that gives you a clear sense of what real flight training feels like in a structured, professional environment.

Each flight begins with a brief, organized ground session covering the basics of flight, the aircraft, and what to expect. Once airborne, you'll take the controls yourself during a one-hour introductory lesson departing from Eastern WV Regional Airport (KMRB).

Flights are conducted in a well-maintained Piper PA-28 and are open to anyone with no prior experience required. The $200 flat rate includes both ground instruction and flight time. Weekend availability fills quickly, so early scheduling is recommended.

Training Aircraft

Training is conducted in a well-maintained Piper PA-28, a stable, predictable platform trusted by flight schools for decades. Its balanced handling, excellent visibility, and forgiving flight characteristics make it ideal for both new pilots and advanced students refining precision and consistency.

The aircraft is IFR-certified, ADS-B equipped, and maintained with a long-term ownership mindset — emphasizing reliability, clean documentation, and consistent operating practices. Students train in a professional, well-organized environment that supports efficient learning at Eastern WV Regional Airport (KMRB).

Whether you're beginning your aviation journey or advancing toward instrument or commercial training, the PA-28 provides a safe, capable, and confidence-building platform that reflects the structured, methodical approach used throughout Central Aviation's training program.

Training Location

All training is conducted at Eastern West Virginia Regional Airport (KMRB), where the aircraft is based and all instruction originates. KMRB provides a professional, towered environment with long runways, low congestion, and excellent IFR procedures — an ideal setting for efficient lessons and real-world skill development.

Central Aviation serves students from Martinsburg WV, Winchester VA, Hagerstown MD, Charles Town WV, and Northern Virginia. Many students choose KMRB for its structured, predictable training environment without the delays, taxi times, or airspace complexity common at larger metropolitan airports.

Andy — Principal Instructor & Operator

Principal Instructor & Operator

Andy is a CFI, CFII, and MEI who brings a calm, methodical approach to flight training. His focus is on disciplined fundamentals, sound aeronautical decision‑making, and developing pilots who are confident, capable, and prepared for real‑world flying.

His instructional style emphasizes clarity, structure, and steady progression from day one.

Whether you're pursuing aviation as a career or flying for personal enjoyment, Andy’s goal is to provide a supportive, professional training experience that builds long‑term proficiency — not just checkride readiness.
